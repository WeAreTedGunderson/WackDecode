Verdict

Option 1 does not try to decode anything. It decides which public leads are traceable enough to pass into Option 2.

Accepted

Official WHACKD contract

Public sources converge on 0xCF8335727B776d190f9D15a54E6B9B9348439eEE. This is the strongest technical artifact for later on-chain analysis.

Accepted with caveat

britbonglogpost.com

RDAP and Wayback verify the domain and capture timing. Public evidence does not authenticate McAfee control.

Accepted as claims

McAfee DMS corpus

Reporting supports that DMS-like claims circulated. It does not verify the alleged 31+ TB cache or a release mechanism.

Guardrail: This report avoids private data, leaked data, credential material, private wallet-owner attribution, and any claim that the DMS is proven.

Accepted Decode Candidates

Accepted means "real enough to analyze later," not "proven to be a DMS."

AC-001 Official WHACKD contract and creation transaction High artifact confidence

Artifact: Contract 0xCF8335727B776d190f9D15a54E6B9B9348439eEE and creation transaction 0x1bb323576cd7dcb12e9f8507a5e298a0136927a486f959e3984cb7cca21ed96b.

Why it matters: It is public, on-chain, and corroborated by the official WHACKD site, Etherscan, CoinMarketCap, and launch reporting.

Current verification: Direct RPC receipt lookup returns status 0x1, to: null, contract address 0xcf8335727b776d190f9d15a54e6b9b9348439eee, block 8943162, and timestamp 2019-11-16 07:25:23 UTC.

Still unverified: No source proves that the contract encodes a payload, key, or post-death trigger.

Next step: Reconstruct contract calls and burn/full-burn events before testing any "1000th transaction" theory.

Sources: SRC-008, SRC-009, SRC-010, SRC-011, SRC-018, SRC-019

AC-002 britbonglogpost.com Wayback capture and RDAP record Strong existence, weak attribution

Artifact: Wayback capture from 2021-06-24 plus Verisign RDAP registration record for britbonglogpost.com.

Why it matters: It verifies that a public countdown artifact circulated after McAfee's death and was publicly linked to WHACKD speculation.

Still unverified: No public source authenticates McAfee control. The domain was registered after death reporting.

Next step: Preserve page content, archive metadata, outbound links, and capture chronology as a web-provenance artifact.

Sources: SRC-014, SRC-015, SRC-016

AC-003 McAfee public DMS / WHACKD claim corpus Claim confidence

Artifact: Public reporting of McAfee statements about a data release, arrest/disappearance, WHACKD, and "if I suicide myself" claims.

Why it matters: The broader DMS theory depends on whether McAfee made DMS-like public claims before death.

Still unverified: Direct original tweet archives are not accepted as primary evidence in this pass, and no source verifies the alleged 31+ TB cache.

Next step: Use this corpus only as a timeline and hypothesis source unless original posts and archives are independently preserved.

Sources: SRC-004, SRC-017, SRC-018, SRC-019, SRC-023
